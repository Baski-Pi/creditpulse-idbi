This video serves as the official **Orientation Session for IDBI Innovate 2026**, a hackathon designed to foster innovation in modern banking by leveraging external expertise from developers and fintech startups (2:36 - 2:58). Hosted by *Sheri* and featuring guest speaker *Prashant Kumar Lanka* (Deputy General Manager, Digital Banking at *IDBI Bank*), the session outlines the bank's vision and the logistics for participants.

### **Hackathon Vision & Goals**
*IDBI Bank* is shifting from its traditional roots toward a modern commercial banking profile. The bank aims to use this hackathon to co-create **scalable, production-ready solutions** using latest industry technologies, APIs, and a sandbox environment (3:42 - 7:34).

### **Problem Statement Tracks**
Participants can choose from five distinct tracks (7:42 - 14:52):
1. **Digital Wealth Management:** AI-powered, 360-degree advisory application for retail/liability customers.
2. **Lead Generation:** A tool to identify prospective customers and match their needs with bank products.
3. **MSME Business Solutions:** Utilizing AI/ML and data sources (like GST/Account Aggregator) to serve the underserved MSME segment.
4. **Credit Utilization & Default Prediction:** Building predictive models to monitor credit health and forecast defaults up to 12 months in advance.
5. **Novel Innovation:** An open track for creative, bank-relevant ideas outside the predefined problem statements.

### **Important Logistics & Roadmap**
*   **Total Prize Pool:** 15 Lakhs (16:25).
*   **Next Milestone:** July 9th is the deadline for registration and prototype submission (17:33).
*   **Refinement Phase:** Shortlisted participants will enter a refined prototype submission phase from July 22nd to July 31st (19:23).
*   **Platform Details:** Teams of up to 4 members can register on the *IDBI Innovate* platform. Submissions require a PPT template, a deployment link, and a GitHub repository link (19:54 - 21:08).
*   **Partners:** The initiative is supported by *AWS* (cloud infrastructure/knowledge partner), *Hack2skill* (platform management), and *ACC* (sandbox support) (15:00 - 16:13).

The Q&A session (21:55 - 38:37) featured several inquiries from participants regarding the *IDBI Innovate 2026* hackathon. Here is the summary of the questions and their corresponding answers:

* **Team Composition:** Teams can consist of members from different companies and cities, with a **maximum size of four members** (21:58 - 22:10, 32:37 - 32:44).
* **Lead Generation Scope:** The tool should initially focus on **existing IDBI bank customers**, with the possibility of extending to new prospects once the solution is refined (22:15 - 23:09).
* **AI/ML Usage:** IDBI currently uses AI/ML in its lending journeys but is looking to **scale up these capabilities** through this hackathon (23:16 - 23:49).
* **NTC/NTB Journeys:** Currently, these journeys are often manual; the bank aims to transition toward more **digital/STP (Straight Through Processing) journeys** (23:54 - 25:03).
* **Eligibility for Solo/Students:** **Individual professionals** and **students/freelancers** are eligible to participate, provided their solutions are scalable and meet the bank's requirements (25:06 - 26:47, 29:12 - 30:24).
* **MSME Rejections:** These typically occur at the **initial application review stage** once the borrower provides necessary data points (26:49 - 27:49).
* **Multiple Tracks:** Teams are restricted to submitting a solution to **only one problem statement** (27:52 - 28:06).
* **MSME Growth Identification:** The bank currently relies on **traditional methods** (balance sheets, turnover) and is looking for digital tools to expedite this identification (28:09 - 29:08).
* **Prototype Submission:** Participants must submit a **working prototype** (deployment link and GitHub repository link) alongside their PPT during the initial submission stage (31:02 - 32:35).
* **Default Prediction Metrics:** Participants can select their own target metrics, but the bank specifically wants to **improve accuracy** and increase the **advance notice period** for predicting defaults (32:50 - 34:13).
* **Data Provisioning:** The bank will provide **mock data** (including transaction, UPI patterns, and MSME financials) during the sandboxing stage for shortlisted participants (34:16 - 35:01, 36:34 - 37:16).
* **Novel Innovation Track:** This is for ideas that are **unrelated to the four specified problem statements** (35:03 - 35:28).
* **Loan Automation:** Current loan processes remain largely manual; the bank is working to **automate these via digital solutions** (37:18 - 38:07).
